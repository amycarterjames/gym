Same routine as last week. They know it. 10 minutes of plyometric warm-up. Focus on form, not speed.

6:05–6:20 Stretch & conditioning ▼
— Your usual routine
6:20–6:35 Floor skills circuit ▼

All together, flowing down the floor in sequence. Self-paced. You circulate and correct. The flow is everything — cartwheels feed into rolls, rolls feed into handstand work.

— Cartwheel snap down tip ▾

Cartwheel with a snap at the end — feet together in the air. Classic move.

— 1 cartwheel one-foot landing tip ▾

Land on one foot only — the other stays lifted. Freeze for 2 seconds. Shows control and balance. Prep for round-off snap.

— 2 cartwheels, last one snap down tip ▾

Two cartwheels in a row, snap the feet together on the last one.

— Handstand forward roll (straight arms) tip ▾

Kick to handstand, hold for a beat, then tuck the chin to chest and let the body roll forward to standing. The key is tucking the chin BEFORE the hands leave the floor.

— Straddle forward roll tip ▾

Legs wide in the roll, don't tuck them. Roll forward, push with hands on the floor to stand up straight — no hands touching yourself at the end. Tests control and core.

— Pike backward roll → front support tip ▾

Backward roll with legs straight and hips bent (pike). Roll over shoulders, place hands, and push to straight-arm front support (plank). Hold 2 sec. Builds shoulder strength and links to back handspring awareness.

— Handstand hops tip ▾

Kick to handstand at the wall. Shift weight from hand to hand — small hops. Builds shoulder proprioception and hand placement comfort. Stay tight — don't let the hips pike.

"Each element builds the next — stay focused."
6:35–6:50 Walkover stations ▼

3 stations, rotate every 5 min. 2 gymnasts each. You spot at Station 1. Back walkovers build the backward confidence needed for round-off back handsprings.

Station 1 — Floor
Tick tocks & kickover to handstand on vertical crashmat · You spot here
– Tick-tock progression → handstand in split spot ▾

Lean back, rock forward (tick-tock motion), build momentum, kick to handstand with one foot on the vertical mat. The mat gives feedback — they feel where their body is in space.

Spotting

One hand on lower back, one on hip. Guide the momentum. The wall/mat does the work.

Station 2 — Soft beam
Walkovers · Self-directed
– Choose: front or back walkover on beam tip ▾

Soft beam means confidence is high. Pick the walkover that feels right. Focus on pushing through the shoulders and looking at the beam as you go over. Soft beam is forgiving — commit fully.

Station 3 — Floor
Backward walkovers · Partner feedback
– Back walkover — full version spot ▾

Stand tall, reach arms overhead, lean back into a back-bend, place one hand then the other on the floor, kick legs over. Land on one foot, then step to standing.

Spotting

One hand on lower back throughout, one on hip or landing leg. Stay in contact — guide the rotation smoothly. Don't lift — just support.

6:50–7:00 Leaps & spins together ▼

All together down the floor. You cue the whole group.

— Split leaps tip ▾

Back leg snaps up to meet the front. Don't reach the front leg forward. Think "split in the air, not a stretch."

— Cat leaps tip ▾

Front leg straight, back knee bent up. Different shape, same explosive take-off. High hips.

— Turning scissor leaps tip ▾

Leap with a quarter turn in the air — swap legs mid-air. Builds body awareness and aerial sense.

— Standing jump full turns tip ▾

Jump and spin a full rotation. Rise first on relevé, THEN turn. Arms pull in tight to speed the turn. Spot a fixed point on the wall.

"Leap high, land light."
7:00–7:20 Round-off & back handspring progressions ▼

3 areas, 2 gymnasts each. You park at Area 3 when spotting is needed. This is your protected block.

Area 1 — Floor
Round-off technique
– Round-off — snap feet together tip ▾

Step, hurdle, cartwheel arms, snap both feet together simultaneously. The snap is everything. Do it slowly first, then build speed.

– Round-off rebound — land, punch, jump tip ▾

After the snap, feet land and immediately punch straight up into a vertical jump. No pause. This trains the reactive punch needed for every skill after a round-off.

"Snap feet together — then punch."
Area 2 — Air track
Round-off into crashmat against wall
– Round-off → sit into crashmat tip ▾

Crashmat propped against the wall. Round-off and drive hips UP — landing in a sitting position against the mat. The wall gives reference. Key drill: teaches hips driving through, NOT throwing the head back.

Watch for

Head throwing back before feet snap — cue "eyes forward until your feet hit." If they're sitting cleanly with back to the mat, they're doing it right.

"Hips drive through — don't throw your head."
Area 3 — Spot here
Round-off progressions into skills
– Round-off → back walkover (confident gymnasts) spot ▾

The rebound from the round-off gives momentum — walkover is often easier here than from standing. Remind them not to pause after landing.

Spotting

One hand on lower back, one ready under the lead leg as it comes over. Guide, don't lift. Step back once they've found their feet.

– Back handspring progressions — beginner spot ▾

Start with handstand flatback into stacked crashmat. Then handstand fall to feet with your spot. Then BHS attempt with heavy support. Never leave her standing watching.

Spotting

One hand on hip, one on shoulder as she goes over. Help her feel the rotation — guide through, don't hold back. Praise every attempt.

"Punch the floor away — don't fall into it."
7:20–7:26 Dive rolls & front somersaults ▼

Trampette work. Progress carefully. You spot here.

Warm-up
Forward roll on trampette
– Forward roll — get comfortable tip ▾

Run-up, step onto trampette, bounce once, roll into crashmat. 1–2 goes before progressing to diver roll.

Main Skill
Diver roll with spot
– Diver roll — drive UP, then curl spot ▾

Run, hurdle onto trampette, take off two feet, drive arms and chest forward and UP (like diving into a pool), tuck chin and roll. Should have a moment of flight.

Spotting

Stand to the side. One hand on upper back/shoulders, one ready under hips to slow descent. Voice: "drive forward, trust it." One at a time. Don't chain to front tuck until diver roll is clean and confident.

"Drive hips UP, then curl."
Progression
Front somersault (tuck) if ready
– Front tuck — only if diver roll is solid tip ▾

After a clean diver roll, progress to tucked front somersault. Only for those with solid body awareness and diver roll control. Don't rush.

"One skill solid before the next."

Safety: Trampette one at a time · Mat stacked high · You spot

7:26–7:28 Handstand game ▼
— Relay teams: hold handstand 5 sec each tip ▾

Divide into two relay teams (3 each). Take turns: kick to handstand at the wall, hold for 5 full seconds with tight body, step down. Tag the next teammate. First team to complete all rotations wins. High energy, competitive, fun way to end on a win.

7:28–7:30 Cool-down ▼
— Quick stretches: straddle, splits tip ▾

30 sec seated straddle, 30 sec splits each side. Keep it moving — wind down heart rate.
