Amy's Gym

Floor · 29 Apr 2026 · 6 gymnasts · 10–13 yrs · 5:45–7:15pm

5:45–5:50 Plyo warm-up ▼
— Tuck jumps — squeeze at the top tip ▾

Jump up, pull both knees to chest at the top. Arms swing up to help lift. Land softly, straight into the next one. Focus on height, not speed.

— Broad jumps down the floor — big power tip ▾

Two feet together, swing arms back then drive forward and up. Land with bent knees, absorb it, then go again. Cover as much floor as possible each jump.

— Ankle bounces — tiny, fast, hot floor tip ▾

Both feet together, stay on toes the whole time. Tiny, fast, springy jumps — barely leaving the ground. No flat feet at all. Trains the spring needed for punching skills.

— Split squat jumps — alternate legs tip ▾

Start in a lunge — one foot forward, one back. Jump straight up, swap legs in the air, land back in a lunge on the other side. Builds single-leg power for leap take-offs. Keep the torso upright.

— Lateral hops over a line tip ▾

Two feet together, hop side to side over a floor line continuously — fast and reactive. Builds the quick foot response needed for punching into skills.

5:50–6:00 Dynamic mobility ▼
— Leg swings front/back then side-to-side (wall) tip ▾

Hold wall with one hand. Swing the outside leg forward and back loosely — let momentum build. Then turn and swing side to side. 10 each direction per leg.

— Big hip circles tip ▾

Hands on hips, feet hip-width. Draw the biggest circle you can with your hips — slow and controlled. 5 each direction.

— Lunge with thoracic twist each step tip ▾

Step into a lunge, rotate the same-side arm and shoulder up toward the ceiling. Look up. Step through, repeat other side. Opens chest and spine for walkovers and bridges.

— Cartwheels down the floor × 2 each way tip ▾

Straight arms, looking at hands, landing one foot then the other in a line. Both directions — prep for beam cartwheels later.

— Handstand holds at wall × 3, 10 sec each tip ▾

Kick up to face the wall (chest toward wall). Squeeze everything — legs together, glutes on, ribs in. Push through the shoulders. Hold 10 sec.

Watch for

Arched lower back — cue them to tuck ribs and squeeze tummy. Bent elbows — push the floor away.

— Kick up to handstand → tip over to bridge × 5 spot ▾

Kick to handstand, slowly lower one leg and tip over into a bridge landing. Push up, then stand. Builds shoulder flexibility and back-bend confidence.

Spotting

Stand to the side. As they tip over, one hand on lower back, one on upper thigh to slow the descent. Guide, don't catch.

6:00–6:10 Stretch & conditioning ▼
— Your usual routine
6:10–6:35 Tumbling — round-offs ▼

All together. Pair up across 3 areas. You circulate — park at area 3 when spotting needed.

Area 1 — Floor
Round-off technique
– Round-off — hurdle, arm swing, snap feet together tip ▾

Break it down: step, hurdle (one foot), cartwheel with the snap — both feet land together simultaneously. Arms drive down and back through. The snap is everything — that's where the power comes from. Do it slowly first, then build speed.

– Round-off rebound — land, punch, jump immediately tip ▾

After the round-off snap, the feet hit the floor and immediately punch up into a vertical jump — arms shoot overhead. No pause. This trains the reactive punch they'll need for every skill that follows a round-off.

"Snap the feet together — then punch."
Area 2 — Air track
Round-off into crashmat against wall
– Round-off → sit into crashmat against wall tip ▾

Crashmat propped against the wall at the end of the air track. Gymnast does a round-off and drives hips through and UP — landing in a sitting/pike position against the mat. The wall gives them a reference so they feel where their body ends up. Teaches hips driving through, NOT throwing the head back. This is the key drill for today.

Watch for

Head throwing back before the feet snap — cue "eyes forward until your feet hit, then look." If they're sitting cleanly with back to the mat, they're doing it right.

– Round-off rebound on air track — build height tip ▾

Air track gives extra spring — great for feeling the rebound. Land, punch, jump. Encourage them to feel how the air track amplifies their punch. More height = more time for the next skill.

"Hips drive through — don't throw your head."
Area 3 — Spot here
Round-off progressions into skills
– Round-off → back walkover (those who have it) spot ▾

The rebound from the round-off gives them the momentum — they often find the walkover easier here than from standing. Remind them not to pause after landing.

Spotting

Stand on the landing side. One hand on lower back, one ready under the lead leg as it comes over. Guide, don't lift. Step back once they've found their feet.

– Front handspring progressions — beginner spot ▾

Start with handstand flatback into a stacked crashmat — gets her comfortable being upside down and falling forward. Then handstand fall to feet with your spot. Then front handspring attempt with a heavy spot. Never leave her standing watching.

Spotting

One hand on the hip, one on the shoulder as she goes over. Help her feel the rotation — guide her through, don't hold her back. Praise every attempt.

"Punch the floor away — don't fall into it."
6:35–7:00 Floor + beam ▼

Pair up across 3 stations. Rotate every 8 min. Park yourself at the beam — that's where spotting matters most.

Station 1 — Soft beam
Walkovers + cartwheels · You spot here
– Confident: walkovers on beam — choose front or back spot ▾

Soft beam so fear is low — encourage them to commit fully. Focus on pushing through the shoulders at the top and looking at the beam on the way over, not the floor.

Spotting

Front walkover: one hand on hip, one ready to support the back of the lead leg as it comes over. Back walkover: one hand on lower back throughout, one on the leg as it drives up. Stay close but let them feel it.

– New to walkovers: bridge kickover progressions spot ▾

Step 1: Bridge on the floor — hold 5 sec, push through shoulders. Step 2: Bridge, then kick one leg up and over to stand. Step 3: Same but from a standing back-bend position. She needs to feel the weight transferring through the shoulders before adding the kick. Don't rush to the beam — nail the floor version first.

Spotting

Kneel beside her in bridge. One hand under her lower back, one ready to catch the kicking leg and guide it over. Say "kick your heel to the ceiling, then follow it." Keep your hand there — she needs to trust the rotation.

– Building: cartwheels on beam tip ▾

Hands one at a time on the beam. Eyes on the beam the whole time — not the floor. Straddle wide, straight arms. Soft beam means a fall is fine — encourage them to commit.

"Push through the shoulders — keep looking at the beam."
Station 2 — Floor leaps
Split leap drill · Self-running
– Skip-leap drill — skip, skip, skip, leap tip ▾

The skip rhythm gets the take-off foot working automatically. On the leap: back leg snaps UP to meet the front — don't reach the front leg forward (that drops the hips). Think "split in the air, not a big kick."

– Leap with partner feedback — does the back leg lift? tip ▾

Partner watches from the side. One thing only to look for: does the back leg snap up to meet the front, or does it drag? Swap after 5 leaps each. Peer feedback works well here — they notice it in each other faster than themselves.

"Lift the back leg to meet the front."
Station 3 — Floor turns
Full turn practice · Self-running
– Full turn — rise first, THEN turn tip ▾

Most common fault: turning before rising onto relevé. Get all the way up on toes first — then initiate the spin. Arms pull in tight to speed the turn. Spot a fixed point on the wall to stop dizziness.

– Partner gives one cue — rise or arms? tip ▾

Partner watches for two things only: did she rise fully before turning? Did her arms pull in tight? Call one thing after each turn. Swap after 5 turns each. Keeps them active and thinking.

"Rise first — then spin."
7:00–7:10 Trampette circuit ▼

All together as a circuit. One gymnast at each station, rotate when you call it. You spot at the trampette.

Station 1 — Trampette
Diver rolls · You spot here
– Forward roll into crashmat — warm-up tip ▾

Run-up, step onto trampette, bounce once, roll into crashmat. Gets them comfortable. 2–3 goes before progressing to the diver roll.

– Diver roll — drive UP, then curl spot ▾

Run, hurdle onto trampette, take off two feet, drive arms and chest forward and UP (like diving into a pool), tuck chin and roll. Should have a moment of flight before hands touch.

Spotting

Stand to the side. If anyone hesitates mid-air, place a hand on their back and guide the roll. Voice: "drive forward, trust it." One at a time — others wait to the side.

"Drive hips UP, then curl."
Station 2 — Air track
Spring & bounce · Self-running
– Tuck jumps — max height, tight tuck at top tip ▾

Use the air track spring to get maximum height. Pull knees all the way to chest at the top. Land and go again immediately. 10 in a row, then rest. Builds the explosive spring needed for everything.

– Straight jumps — punch and reach, stay tight tip ▾

Arms drive up overhead, body stays completely straight and squeezed. No piking at the hips. Land on two feet, punch again immediately. Feel how the air track gives back what you put in.

"The track gives back what you put in — punch hard."
Station 3 — Floor
Handstand forward rolls · Self-running
– Handstand forward roll — kick up, tuck chin, roll out tip ▾

Kick to handstand (or as close as they can), hold for a beat, then tuck the chin to chest and let the body roll forward to standing. The key is tucking the chin BEFORE the hands leave the floor — otherwise they'll crash. Land on two feet and stand up cleanly.

Watch for

Those who aren't reaching a full handstand yet — let them work from a pike position (bent hips), still tucking the chin and rolling. The chin tuck is non-negotiable for safety.

– Tuck chin drill — just the roll-out tip ▾

For anyone nervous: start from a standing forward fold, place hands on floor, tuck chin, and just roll forward to standing without the handstand. Gets the chin-tuck habit in place before adding the inversion.

"Tuck your chin before your hands leave the floor."
7:10–7:12 Routine relay — together ▼
— Chain: split leap → full turn → round-off → walkover or cartwheel → finish pose tip ▾

Each gymnast does the chain in one go across the floor. Doesn't need to be perfect — the point is connecting skills with rhythm and performing with intent. Hold the finish pose for 2 full seconds.

— Run it twice — one cue per girl on the second run tip ▾

First run: just go, no feedback. Second run: one specific thing per gymnast based on what you saw. Keep it positive and simple — "more height on that leap" or "punch harder after the round-off."

"Connect it — one routine, not five separate skills."
7:12–7:15 Cool-down ▼
— Seated straddle — 60 sec tip ▾

Legs wide, toes pointed, hands on floor in front. Hinge from the hips — don't round the back. Walk hands forward slowly. Breathe out on each exhale to release deeper.

— Splits L, R, middle — 45 sec each tip ▾

Square hips for left and right splits — the back hip tends to open out. For middle split, lean forward and support on hands. Let gravity do the work.

— Hip flexor lunge hold — 30 sec each side tip ▾

Back knee on floor, front foot forward. Tuck the pelvis under and drive hips forward. Deep stretch in the front of the back hip. Great for leap power.

— Bridge × 2 → push to stand tip ▾

Lie on back, feet flat near hips, push up into bridge. Hold 5 sec — push through the shoulders and open the chest. Rock slightly and push through hands to stand.

Always Safety ▼
Clear the air track before anyone goes
Trampette: one at a time, mat stacked high
Handstand forward rolls: chin MUST tuck before hands leave floor
Wrist pain → stop tumbling, move to leaps or flexibility
