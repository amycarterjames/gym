Floor session 🌊

6 gymnasts · 10–13 yrs · 90 min · Tap block to open · Tap drill for tips

0–5 min Plyo warm-up ▼
— Tuck jumps — squeeze at the top tip ▾

Jump up, pull both knees to chest at the top. Arms swing up to help lift. Land softly, straight into the next one. Focus on height, not speed.

— Broad jumps down the floor — big power tip ▾

Two feet together, swing arms back then drive forward and up. Land with bent knees, absorb it, then go again. Cover as much floor as possible each jump.

— Ankle bounces — tiny, fast, hot floor tip ▾

Both feet together, stay on toes the whole time. Tiny, fast, springy jumps — barely leaving the ground. Like the floor is burning. No flat feet at all. Great for Achilles and calf elasticity — directly trains the spring they need for punching skills.

— Split squat jumps — alternate legs tip ▾

Start in a lunge — one foot forward, one back. Jump straight up, swap legs in the air, land back in a lunge on the other side. Repeat continuously. Builds single-leg power for leap take-offs. Keep the torso upright.

— Lateral hops over a line tip ▾

Stand beside a floor line (or tape, or imaginary). Two feet together, hop side to side over it continuously — fast and reactive. Builds the quick foot response needed for punching into skills and landing with control.

5–15 min Dynamic mobility ▼
— Leg swings front/back then side-to-side (wall) tip ▾

Hold wall with one hand. Swing the outside leg forward and back loosely — don't force it, let momentum build. Then turn and swing side to side. 10 each direction per leg. Warms up the hip for leaps and walkovers.

— Big hip circles tip ▾

Hands on hips, feet hip-width. Draw the biggest circle you can with your hips — slow and controlled. 5 each direction. Mobilises the lower back and hip flexors.

— Lunge with thoracic twist each step tip ▾

Step into a lunge, plant the front foot, then rotate the same-side arm and shoulder up toward the ceiling. Look up. Step through, repeat other side. Down the floor and back. Opens chest and spine for back walkovers and bridges.

— Cartwheels down the floor × 2 each way tip ▾

This IS a warm-up drill. Focus on straight arms, looking at hands, and landing one foot then the other in a line. Both directions — this is functional prep for beam cartwheels later.

— Handstand holds at wall × 3, 10 sec each tip ▾

Kick up to face the wall (chest toward wall, not back). Squeeze everything — legs together, glutes on, ribs in, long neck. Push through the shoulders. Hold 10 sec. Rest. Repeat 3 times.

👁 Watch for

Arched lower back ("banana") — cue them to tuck ribs and squeeze tummy. Bent elbows — remind them to push the floor away.

— Kick up to handstand → tip over to bridge × 5 spot ▾

Kick to handstand, then slowly lower one leg and tip the whole body over into a bridge landing. Push the bridge up, then stand. Builds shoulder flexibility and back-bend confidence.

🤝 Spotting

Stand to the side. As they tip over, place one hand on their lower back and one on their upper thigh to slow the descent. Don't catch — guide. Once in bridge, encourage them to push up themselves.

15–25 min Stretch & conditioning ▼
— Your usual routine
25–75 min 3 stations — swap every 15 min ▼

2 gymnasts per station. You float and spot. 3 rounds total.

1 — Tumble track
Round-off & handsprings
– Round-off → land → immediate vertical jump (rebound drill) tip ▾

The point is to train a fast, reactive punch off the floor after landing — not to do a pretty round-off. Land with feet together, arms shoot up, jump immediately. Don't let them pause and think.

– Round-off → back walkover (those who have it) spot ▾

Round-off to rebound, then drive straight into the back walkover. The rebound gives them the momentum they need — they often find it easier off a round-off than standing.

🤝 Spotting

Stand on the landing side. One hand on lower back, one ready under the lead leg as it comes over. Guide, don't lift. Step back once they've found their feet.

– Front handspring — punch, don't sit spot ▾

The most common fault: sitting into the hurdle before the handspring. Cue them to keep the hips driving forward and UP off the board/floor. The punch is a two-footed stamp, not a step.

🤝 Spotting

Stand on the side they land toward. As they go into the handstand phase, place one hand on the hip and one on the shoulder. Help them feel the rotation. Don't hold them back — guide them over.

– Connect round-off to front handspring if confident tip ▾

Only for gymnasts with a solid front handspring already. The round-off gives the punch energy — remind them not to pause between the two skills. It should feel like one continuous move.

"Punch the floor away — don't fall into it."
2 — Floor + soft beam
Walkovers, turns & leaps
– Confident: walkovers on the beam spot ▾

Beam is soft so low fear factor — encourage them to really commit. Focus on pushing through the shoulders at the top and looking at the beam on the way over, not the floor.

🤝 Spotting

Stand beside the beam. For front walkover: one hand on hip, one ready to support the back of the lead leg as it comes over. For back walkover: one hand on lower back throughout, one on the leg as it drives up. Stay close but let them feel it.

– Building: cartwheels on the beam tip ▾

Hands one at a time on the beam, not both at once. Eyes on the beam the whole time — not the floor. Straddle wide, straight arms. The soft beam means a fall is fine — encourage them to just go for it.

– Floor: full turn on one foot — rise first, THEN turn tip ▾

The most common fault: turning before rising onto relevé. Cue them to get all the way up on their toes first — then initiate the spin. Arms pull in tight to speed the turn. Spot a fixed point on the wall to stop dizziness.

– Split leaps — skip, skip, skip, leap tip ▾

The skip rhythm gets the take-off foot working automatically. On the leap: the back leg snaps UP to meet the front — don't reach the front leg forward (that drops the hips). Think "split in the air, not a big kick."

"Lift the back leg to meet the front."
3 — Trampette
Diver rolls — fun + front sommy prep
– Forward roll into crashmat — confidence warm-up tip ▾

Just a basic run-up, step onto trampette, bounce once, roll into the crashmat. Gets them comfortable with the equipment and removes fear before the diver roll. Let them do 2–3 of these before progressing.

– Diver roll off trampette: dive arms-first, tuck, roll spot ▾

Run, hurdle onto trampette, take off from two feet, drive arms and chest forward and UP (like diving into a pool), then tuck the chin and roll. They should have a moment of flight before their hands touch. Not a front drop — they need to get over their hands.

🤝 Spotting

Stand to the side of the trampette. If anyone hesitates mid-air or goes crooked, be ready to place a hand on their back and guide the roll. Don't intervene unless needed — hesitation is normal at first. Encourage with voice: "drive forward, trust it."

– Build height and tuck tightness each go tip ▾

Once they're comfortable with the diver roll shape, encourage more height off the trampette. Cue: "jump UP before you go forward." The higher they go, the more time to tuck. This is directly building the shape they'll need for a front somersault.

– Most advanced: front tuck shape into crashmat tip ▾

For gymnasts with a confident, high diver roll only. Same take-off, but tuck tighter and faster — they should feel the rotation happening in the air before they hit the mat. Not landing on feet yet — just experiencing the feeling. Praise the attempt, not the landing.

"Drive hips UP, then curl."
One at a time. Others line up to the side, never behind.
75–85 min Routine relay — together ▼
— Chain: split leap → full turn → round-off → walkover or cartwheel → finish pose tip ▾

Each gymnast does the chain in one go across the floor. It doesn't need to be perfect — the point is connecting skills with rhythm and performing with intent. Hold the finish pose for 2 full seconds.

— Run it twice — one cue per girl on the second run tip ▾

First run: just do it, no feedback. Second run: give each gymnast one specific thing to focus on based on what you saw. Keep it positive and simple — "more height on that leap" or "punch harder after the round-off."

"Connect it — make it look like one routine, not five separate skills."
85–90 min Cool-down ▼
— Seated straddle — 60 sec tip ▾

Legs wide, toes pointed, hands on floor in front. Hinge from the hips — don't round the back. Walk hands forward slowly. Hold for the full 60 sec, breathing out on each exhale to release deeper.

— Splits L, R, middle — 45 sec each tip ▾

Square hips for left and right splits — the back hip tends to open out. Hips stacked, toes pointed. For middle split, lean forward and support on hands. Don't push — let gravity do the work after a good warm-up.

— Hip flexor lunge hold tip ▾

Back knee on floor, front foot forward. Tuck the pelvis under and drive the hips forward — you should feel a deep stretch in the front of the back hip. Great for leap take-off power and preventing tight hip flexors. 30–45 sec each side.

— Bridge × 2 → push to stand tip ▾

Lie on back, feet flat near hips, push up into bridge. Hold 5 sec — push through the shoulders and open the chest. Then rock slightly and push through the hands to stand. This is also wrist extension maintenance — important for handspring work.

Always Safety ▼
Clear the tumble track before anyone goes
Trampette: one at a time, mat stacked high
Wrist pain → stop tumbling, move to leaps or flexibility
Have a great session 🌊
